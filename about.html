About

Translating between the wet lab and the model

Cultured Machine exists because the most exciting work in biology right now happens where it meets AI — and almost nobody explains it in plain language.

I'm a bioprocess engineer (Dipl.-Ing., KIT) who spent years in the lab working on protein characterization, formulation, and purification, with peer-reviewed publications in protein science. I later retrained in data science and now work in applied AI, helping bring machine learning into a heavy industry. That path — from the bench to the model and back — is exactly the translation this site is about.

I write here for three kinds of reader: the biologist who wants to understand what these AI models actually do, the technologist who's curious about biology but bounces off the jargon, and anyone simply curious about what's happening at the intersection of AI and biotech. If that's you, you're in the right place.

What you'll find

Two things, both free and open. Explainers take one concept — protein folding, language models for proteins, generative drug design — and make it click with a visualization you can poke at. The weekly digest rounds up the handful of Biotech+AI stories that genuinely matter, in plain English, with a line on why each one counts.

How the site is made

I believe in being transparent about process. The news digest starts from an automated sweep of trusted biotech and AI sources each week; the raw headlines are drafted into plain English with the help of an AI assistant, then I review, fact-check, and edit every issue before it publishes. The explainers are researched and written by me, with AI used as a drafting and illustration aid — never as the final word. Nothing goes out that I haven't checked.
