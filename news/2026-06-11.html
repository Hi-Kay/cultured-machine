Protein design

A new generative model designs binders for "undruggable" targets

A research team reported a diffusion model that designs small proteins to latch onto disease targets long considered impossible to drug, with a meaningful fraction working on the first lab test. The approach skips the slow trial-and-error screening that normally eats years.

Why it matters: if generative design keeps improving its hit rate, the bottleneck in drug discovery shifts from "can we find a molecule?" to "which disease do we point it at?" · Source: [link]

Funding

An AI-for-bioprocessing startup raises a large Series B

A company using machine learning to optimize how biologic drugs are manufactured — predicting yields and catching deviations in real time — closed a sizeable round. Manufacturing, not discovery, is increasingly where AI money is flowing.

Why it matters: discovery gets the headlines, but most of a drug's cost and risk lives in manufacturing. AI that de-risks the factory floor is a quietly huge market. · Source: [link]

Foundation models

A bigger protein language model is released openly

An open-weights protein language model arrived with more parameters and better performance on predicting function and stability. Open releases let academic and startup labs build without negotiating commercial licenses.

Why it matters: open foundation models are doing for biology what they did for text — lowering the barrier so small teams can compete with big pharma's data advantage. · Source: [link]

Regulation

Regulators publish draft guidance on AI in drug development

A major regulator released draft guidance on how AI/ML models used in drug discovery and trials should be validated and documented. It signals that "the model told us so" won't be enough — transparency and reproducibility will be expected.

Why it matters: clear rules are what let AI tools move from flashy demos into approved products. This is the boring infrastructure that decides which companies scale. · Source: [link]

Worth a read

A thoughtful critique: where AI-for-bio is overhyped

A widely shared essay argued that wet-lab validation, not model cleverness, is still the rate-limiting step — and that data quality beats model size in biology. A useful counterweight to the breathless coverage.

Why it matters: knowing the limits is how you spot the real opportunities. The gap between a benchmark and a working therapy is where careers and companies are made. · Source: [link]
